Rooted.

An app to help new plant owners learn more about how to care for their plants.

Background

Project for Human-Computer Interaction Class 2022

Course Instructor: Matthew Bietz

Duration: 10 weeks

Role: UI/UX Designer

Team: Hannah Limary, Carly Chan, Regina Tambunan, Kevin Tsai, Rohan Hemrajani

Problem Overview

The Problem: With everyone staying indoors, there has been a rise in people interested in becoming plant parents. However, oftentimes people do not know where to begin when it comes to taking care of plants or feel unsupported with their scheduling to properly care for their plants.

Problem Statement: How might we educate plant owners about their plants and how to care for them?

Research

In order to better understand the needs of plant owners, we needed to gather more insights into what plant parents really want/need. To do this, we conducted a total of 12 interviews; the people interviewed had varying levels of experience ranging from no experience to multiple years if caring for plants.

After conducting the interviews, we reconvened as a team to assess our findings and identify key insights using a miro board. With each of our individual participants of varying plant experience labeled in a different color, we were able to identify trends and needs across different user types.

Key Insights

Individuals use a variety of mediums to learn and understand how to better care for their plants.

90% of all interviewees referred to online resources for finding plant information.

Plant owners don't want to keep a strict schedule for plant care, but still want to be consistent.

8 out of the 10 plant owners we interviewed didn't have or want to implement a strict schedule for plant care

Novice plant owners fixate their care attention on water and sunlight.

4/5 novice plant owner interviewees described processes of plant care as “water and sunlight”
